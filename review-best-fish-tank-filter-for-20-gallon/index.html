Buyer's Guide

Filter Type


When choosing the best fish tank filter for a 20-gallon aquarium, consider the type of filter: internal, external, or canister. Internal filters are compact and sit inside the tank, making them great for smaller setups. External filters provide more powerful filtration and are often quieter, but they require more space.

Flow Rate


The flow rate is crucial for maintaining water quality. Look for filters with a flow rate of at least 5 times the tank volume per hour. For a 20-gallon tank, aim for a filter with a flow rate of around 100 GPH.

Media Capacity


Filters with larger media capacity allow for more effective biological and mechanical filtration. If you have a heavily stocked tank, opt for filters that offer customization for media types to suit your specific needs.

Ease of Maintenance


Choose filters that are easy to clean and maintain. Features like quick-release mechanisms or modular designs can save you time and effort during maintenance.

Noise Level


If you're sensitive to noise, especially in a living area, consider filters known for quiet operation. Internal filters tend to be quieter than external or canister filters, but reviews can give insight into noise levels.

FAQ

What size filter do I need for a 20-gallon tank?
For a 20-gallon tank, choose a filter rated for at least 100 GPH. This ensures sufficient water turnover, maintaining a healthy environment for your fish.

How often should I change the filter media?
Typically, filter media should be replaced every 4 to 6 weeks, but this can vary based on bioload. Monitor your water quality and adjust your maintenance routine accordingly.

Can I use a filter for both freshwater and saltwater?
Yes, many filters can be used for both freshwater and saltwater tanks, but ensure they are made from materials resistant to corrosion from saltwater.

What is the difference between internal and external filters?
Internal filters are compact and sit inside the tank, while external filters are placed outside and often provide stronger filtration. External filters usually allow for larger media capacities.

How do I know if my filter is working properly?
Check for water movement in the tank and monitor water quality. If you notice a decline in water clarity or an increase in ammonia levels, it may indicate that the filter is not functioning effectively.
