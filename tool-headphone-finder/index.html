Frequently Asked Questions

What are open-ear headphones?
Open-ear headphones sit outside your ear canal, using bone conduction or directional audio to deliver sound while letting you hear your surroundings. Perfect for running, cycling, or office work where situational awareness matters.
Are open-ear headphones good for working out?
Yes. They stay put during intense movement, do not trap sweat in your ear canal (reducing infection risk), and let you hear traffic or gym announcements. Most are IPX5+ water resistant.
Do open-ear headphones have good bass?
Modern open-ear headphones like OpenRock have significantly improved bass response. They will not match sealed over-ear headphones, but the bass is surprisingly punchy for the form factor.
Can people around me hear my open-ear headphones?
At moderate volumes, sound leakage is minimal. Directional audio technology focuses sound toward your ear. At max volume in a quiet room, people nearby may hear faint audio.
How long do wireless headphone batteries last?
Most quality wireless headphones last 6-10 hours per charge. With a charging case, total battery life extends to 24-48 hours. Fast charging gives 1-2 hours of playback from a 10-minute charge.
