The challenge with choosing a smartwatch for kids is that many parents focus solely on fun features like games and cameras, overlooking critical safety and communication capabilities.

Why GPS Tracking Accuracy Matters

GPS tracking is a non-negotiable feature for kids’ smartwatches. You want solid accuracy to ensure you know where your child is at all times. Look for watches with GPS accuracy within 10 meters. A reliable GPS system can be the difference between finding your child quickly or wondering if they’ve wandered too far.

Imagine a scenario where your child is at a park and says they’re on one side while you’re on the other. If the GPS is off, finding them could take longer than necessary, leading to unnecessary anxiety. Smartwatches with accurate GPS tracking can provide real-time updates, giving you confidence in your child's whereabouts.

Battery Life: What You Really Need

Battery life is another critical factor. Look for smartwatches that can last at least two days on a single charge. Kids often forget to charge their devices, and a dead watch means no tracking or communication.

Consider this: If your child wears the watch to school and it dies by lunch, you lose the ability to communicate or track them for the rest of the day. A longer battery life ensures the watch remains functional during all of your child’s activities, from school to playdates.

Durability and Waterproofing: Built to Last

Kids can be rough on their belongings, so durability is essential. Look for a smartwatch with an IP68 or higher waterproof rating. This means it can withstand splashes, spills, and even submersion in water.

Picture this: Your child is at a birthday party and ends up in the pool. A smartwatch that’s not waterproof could be ruined, but one with a solid waterproof rating will keep working, allowing you to maintain contact. Durability helps ensure that the watch lasts through all of life's little accidents.

Ease of Use for Kids: Intuitive Design Matters

A smartwatch should be user-friendly. Look for options with simple interfaces and a responsive touchscreen. Children can easily become frustrated with complicated menus or slow performance.

For instance, if a child is trying to send a quick message or call you in an emergency, they won’t want to deal with a confusing interface. The more intuitive the design, the more likely your child will use the smartwatch as intended, ensuring they can communicate with you when they need to.

Parental Controls: Keeping It Safe

Effective parental controls are essential for safety. Look for smartwatches that allow parents to manage contacts, monitor activity, and set screen time limits. This feature ensures your child can only communicate with trusted people.

Imagine a situation where your child receives a message from an unknown number. With robust parental controls, you can prevent that from happening altogether. Monitoring capabilities let you keep an eye on their usage and ensure the device remains a safe tool for communication.

What Most People Get Wrong

1. Overemphasis on Fun Features: Parents often focus on games and cameras, forgetting that the primary function of these devices should be safety and communication.

2. Ignoring Subscription Costs: Some watches require monthly subscriptions for full functionality. This can add up, so consider the long-term costs before buying.

3. Underestimating Battery Life Needs: Many parents think a watch that lasts a day is good enough, but a longer battery life ensures reliability, especially for active kids.

4. Neglecting to Research App Compatibility: Some watches only work with specific apps or platforms. Ensure that the watch you choose is compatible with your smartphone’s operating system.

5. Assuming All GPS is Created Equal: Not all GPS systems are equally accurate. Check reviews and specifications to ensure you’re getting one that meets your needs.

The Bottom Line: Which One Is Right for You?


  • For Safety-Focused Parents: The Bark Watch is your best bet, thanks to its superior safety features and real-time tracking capabilities. It’s perfect for parents who prioritize monitoring and communication.
  • For Older Kids Wanting More Functionality: Consider the COSMO JrTrack 5. It’s great for tech-savvy kids who want a smartwatch experience similar to a smartphone, complete with video calling and extra apps.
  • For Younger Kids: The Gabb Watch 3e offers a durable design and essential features like SOS buttons and parent-controlled contacts. It’s the ideal choice for kids just starting to explore smart devices.

No matter your needs, there's a smartwatch out there that can fit your family perfectly. Just remember to focus on safety, battery life, and ease of use, and you'll make a decision you won't regret.
