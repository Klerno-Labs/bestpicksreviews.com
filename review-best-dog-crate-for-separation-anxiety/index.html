Buyer's Guide

Crate Size


Choosing the right size crate is essential for comfort. A crate should be large enough for your dog to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably, but not too large that they can use one side as a bathroom.

Material Durability


Select a crate made from durable materials. Metal crates are generally sturdier than plastic ones, especially for dogs prone to chewing or scratching.

Escape-Proof Features


For dogs with severe separation anxiety, look for escape-proof features like reinforced locks and heavy-duty construction to ensure your pet's safety.

Portability


If you plan to travel with your dog, consider a lightweight and collapsible crate that’s easy to transport.

Ease of Cleaning


Features like removable trays and water-resistant materials make it easier to clean up after accidents, which is crucial for anxious dogs.

FAQ

What size crate is best for my dog?


The best crate size allows your dog to stand up, turn around, and lie down comfortably. Measure your dog from nose to tail and add a few inches for the right fit.

How do I help my dog adjust to a crate?


Start by introducing your dog to the crate gradually. Use treats and positive reinforcement to create a positive association, and never use the crate as a punishment.

Are soft-sided crates safe for anxious dogs?


Soft-sided crates can be safe for dogs with mild anxiety, but they may not withstand an aggressive chewer. For severe anxiety, consider a more durable option.

What features should I look for in a crate for separation anxiety?


Look for a secure, escape-proof design, comfort features like a soft bed, and easy access points. A crate that mimics a den can also help anxious dogs feel more secure.

How can I prevent my dog from escaping the crate?


Choose a crate with reinforced construction and secure locking mechanisms. Training your dog to feel comfortable in their crate can also reduce attempts to escape.
