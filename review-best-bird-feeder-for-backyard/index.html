Buyer's Guide

Feeder Type


There are several types of feeders: tube feeders, platform feeders, and window feeders. Tube feeders attract smaller birds like finches, while platform feeders can accommodate larger birds.

Capacity


If you want to spend less time refilling, go for feeders with larger capacities. Some feeders can hold up to 5 lbs, which is great for busy bird watchers.

Material Durability


Choose feeders made from durable materials like metal or polycarbonate. These tend to withstand the elements better and last longer.

Squirrel Resistance


If you live in an area with squirrels, consider feeders designed to be squirrel-resistant. Look for all-metal constructions or feeders with baffles.

Ease of Cleaning


Feeders that are easy to take apart and clean can save you a lot of hassle. Look for removable trays or easy-to-access compartments.

FAQ

What type of bird feeder is best for my backyard?


The best bird feeder depends on the types of birds you want to attract. Tube feeders are great for finches, while platform feeders attract a wider variety.

How often should I refill my bird feeder?


It depends on the feeder's capacity and the number of birds visiting. A larger feeder may only need refilling every couple of weeks, while smaller ones might need weekly attention.

What seeds should I use for different birds?


Sunflower seeds are great for most birds, while nyjer seeds attract finches. Mixed seeds can attract a variety of species, but check for the specific preferences of your local birds.

How can I prevent squirrels from accessing my feeder?


Using squirrel-proof feeders, adding baffles, or placing feeders in hard-to-reach areas can help keep squirrels away. Some people even use cayenne pepper in their seed mix to deter them.

What maintenance is required for bird feeders?


Regular cleaning is crucial to prevent mold and disease. Most feeders should be cleaned at least once a month, especially during the warmer months.
