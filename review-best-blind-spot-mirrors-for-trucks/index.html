Buyer’s Guide

When selecting the best blind spot mirrors for trucks, there are several technical factors to consider. The right choice depends heavily on the size of your vehicle and how you drive.

Lens Size and Shape


The size of the convex lens directly impacts the field of view. Smaller mirrors (around 2 inches) are excellent for passenger cars and smaller pickups, offering a wide-angle view without much distortion. However, for larger trucks and semi-trucks, a larger lens (3 to 6 inches) is often necessary to capture the massive blind spots created by the trailer. Shapes also vary; round mirrors are standard and provide a full circular view, while rectangular or oval shapes can maximize the width of the viewing area, particularly useful for checking lane changes.

Mounting Method (Adhesive vs. Bracket)


For standard trucks and SUVs, adhesive-backed convex mirrors are the most convenient option. They typically use 3M automotive-grade adhesive that sticks to the glass or metal of your existing mirror housing. However, for heavy-duty commercial trucks, stick-ons can fail due to vibration and the sheer weight of the trailer. In these cases, a bracket or L-bracket mounted mirror (like the Tow Pro models) is superior because it is mechanically fastened, ensuring the mirror stays put even under rough conditions.

Durability and Water Resistance


Truck mirrors are exposed to the elements 24/7. High-quality mirrors must be waterproof to prevent the adhesive from failing or the glass from fogging up. Look for "outdoor-rated" adhesive specifically. Additionally, for off-road or rugged use, a frame around the mirror (like the Frienda model) can protect the pivot mechanism from dirt and damage, extending the lifespan of the product significantly.

Field of View and Visibility


The goal of a blind spot mirror is to eliminate blind spots. When reading reviews, pay attention to the magnification factor. A lens that magnifies images 3x or more than the standard side mirror is usually the best option. This allows you to see a car that might be two lanes over, giving you ample time to react safely. Frameless designs often offer the widest field of view because they don't block any portion of the mirror surface.

Price and Value


You can find excellent blind spot mirrors for as little as $5, making them an affordable safety upgrade. However, specialized industrial mirrors for semis can cost upwards of $40 due to the materials used (stainless steel) and the mounting hardware required. Evaluate whether you need a cheap, temporary solution or a permanent, heavy-duty investment for your fleet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size blind spot mirror do I need for a truck?

The size you need depends on your truck's dimensions. For standard pickup trucks, a 2 to 3-inch convex mirror is usually sufficient. However, for larger semi-trucks and larger trailers, you will likely need a larger mirror, specifically the 6 to 8.5-inch models available for center mounting or side mounting, to ensure you can see vehicles in your large blind spots.

Do blind spot mirrors actually help with truck safety?

Yes, blind spot mirrors are one of the most effective safety upgrades you can make. They utilize a convex lens to bend light and create a wider viewing angle, allowing you to see objects that are often invisible in standard flat mirrors. They significantly reduce the risk of accidents during lane changes, merging, and backing up.

How do I install a blind spot mirror on a truck mirror?

Most stick-on mirrors are tool-free. Start by cleaning the surface of your existing truck mirror with rubbing alcohol to remove oils and dirt. Once dry, peel the backing off the adhesive and press the mirror firmly onto the desired location. You may need to adjust the angle using your hand or a cloth for a few minutes until the image is clear and centered.

What is the best adhesive for blind spot mirrors?

The best adhesive for these mirrors is usually 3M automotive-grade adhesive or a similar weather-resistant adhesive. Ensure the mirror you purchase specifically mentions "outdoor/water-rated" adhesive, as this ensures the mirror won't fall off during rain, snow, or extreme heat.

Are convex blind spot mirrors legal?

Yes, convex blind spot mirrors are legal in all 50 states. They are considered a standard safety accessory, similar to your existing side mirrors. However, it is important that you do not modify your existing factory mirrors in a way that violates vehicle inspection laws (such as removing parts or using illegal tinting).

Can I put a blind spot mirror on any type of truck?

Most blind spot mirrors are universal fit and can be stuck onto almost any flat or slightly curved truck mirror. However, for trucks with very heavily textured mirrors or if you need a very specific mounting point (like the center of a massive semi-trailer), you may need to look for specific models that include an L-bracket or mounting hardware to ensure a secure fit.
